    Sorrowing , the Nine beneath yon blasted yew
    Shed the soft drops of pity's holy dew;3
    Shed the bright drops of Pity's holy dew;4
    Mute are their tuneful tongues, extinct their fires;
    Yet not in silence sleep their silver lyres;
    To the bleak gale they vibrate sad and slow,
    In deep accordance to a Nation's woe.
    Ye, who ere-while for Cook's illustrious brow5
    Ye, who ere while for COOK's illustrious brow6
    Pluck'd the green laurel, and the oaken bough,
    Hung the gay garlands on the trophied oars,
    And pour'd his fame along a thousand shores,
    Strike the slow death-bell!---weave the sacred verse,
    And strew the cypress o'er his honour'd hearse;
    In sad procession wander round the shrine,
    And weep him mortal, whom ye sung divine!
    Say first, what Power inspir'd his dauntless breast 7
    With scorn of danger and inglorious rest, 8
    To quit imperial London's gorgeous domes,9
    Where, deck'd in thousand tints, young Pleasure roams;10
    In cups of summer-ice her nectar pours,11
    Or twines, 'mid wint'ry snows, her roseate bowers;12
    Say first, what Pow'r inspir'd his dauntless breast 13
    With scorn of danger and inglorious rest, 14
    To quit imperial London's gorgeous plains,15
    Where, rob'd in thousand tints, bright Pleasure reigns;16
    In cups of summer-ice her nectar pours,17
    And twines, 'mid wint'ry snows, her roseate bow'rs?18
    Where the warm Orient loads Britannia's gales19
    With all the incense of Sabæan vales;20
    Where soft Italia's silken sons prolong21
    The lavish cadence of the artful song;22
    Where Beauty moves with undulating grace,23
    Calls the sweet blush to wanton o'er her face,24
    On each fond Youth her soft artillery tries,25
    Aims her light smile, and rolls her frolic eyes?26
