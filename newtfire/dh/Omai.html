    Part 1

    Scene 1

    [ A Morai [2] A temple, or burying place. in Otaheite by Moon-light ]

    [ Otoo discovered making Invocations to the Genii of his Ancestors for their Assistance to fix his Son on the Throne. ]

    [ Recitative]

    Otoo :

    1 Hail to this awful place! sacred Fiatoo-ka [3] A repository for the dead hail!

    2 Where Otaheitean Cheifs in everlasting moe [4] Sleep lie; warriors renown'd,

    3 On Eimeo's [5] One of the Society Islands, generally at war with Otaheite sanguine plains with hard=fought victory crown'd;

    4 Tho' here in death ye moulder, yet the royal line can never fail,

    5 Preserv'd by Towha's power divine;

    6 And the illustrious name,

    7 By glorious actions dear to fame,

    8 In my belov'd Omai shall never die.

    [ Air I]

    Otoo :

    9 God of Bolabola [6] Another of the Society Islands, the most ferocious in war, conquerors of Ulitea, which was anciently subject to Omai's ancestors. hear!

    10 Accept this plantain, yam, and hog, well roasted,

    11 Offerings to they Godship dear,

    12 With mahee [7] Bread sweet as ever boasted;

    13 And while thus I lowly bend,

    14 Let my humble suit ascend.

    [ The sacrifice blazes up.]

    15 The flames arise,

    16 Blest sacrifice!

    17 Towha snuffs the favour;

    18 Propitious sign

    19 Of grace benign,

    20 Sure token of his favor.

    [ Recitative]

    21 Say, shall my son omai reign?

    22 Great Towha now an answer deign.

    [ Towha assumes the Appearance of a Chief Mourner.]

    [ Recitative]

    Otoo :

    [ appalled]

    23 My quiv'ring flesh, my limbs bedew'd all o'er,

    24 Each feeble sense---my eyes---my voice---no more!

    [ falls prostrate]

    [ Air II]

    Towha :

    25 Spirits of peace that hover round,

    26 Oh, chear his mortal fight!

    27 Dispel with sweet responsive sound

    28 The horrors of the night.

    [ Celestial music]

    29 My aerial band, ar't ready?

    Chorus of Spirits :

    30 Ready.

    Towha :

    31 To run, to swim, to fly, at my command?

    Chorus of spirits :

    32 Command.

    Towha :

    33 To run?

    Chorus of spirits :

    34 To run

    Towha :

    35 To swim?

    Chorus of spirits :

    36 To swim.

    Towha :

    37 To fly?

    Chorus of spirits :

    38 To fly.

    Towha :

    39 At my command?

    Chorus of spirits :

    40 Command.

    [ Spirits Appear]

    Towha :

    41 His darling son with motion soft as Maoaian [8] Western breezes gales, hither convey

    Chorus of Spirits :

    42 Away!

    [ Disappears]

    [ Soft Music, and Omai brought in]

    [ Omai is brought in]

    [ Air III]

    Chorus (Spirits) :

    43 Happy father---Otaheite's heir!

    [ To Otoo and Omai]

    Otoo :

    44 Otoo's hope,

    Towha :

    45 And Towha's care.

    [ Recitative]

    Towha :

    46 My precepts still from solly shall direct him,

    47 This potent talisman from harm protect him.

    [ Places the talisman by the side of Omai -- Thunder -- Britannia is seen holding Londina]

    [ Britannia speaks]

    Britannia :

    48 Mark, votive Islander, thy fate is mine, for mine.

    49 The Queen of Isles, the mistress of the main!

    50 Upon my sea-girt shore, by Neptune fenc'd,

    51 Kind greeting, pleasure, welcome sweet receive;

    52 Still shall my sons, by Cook's example taught,

    53 Thy new-found world protect and humanize.

    54 In soft alliance bound, this British maid

    55 Be thine, and Love, a radiant throne shall fix

    56 Firm as my rock, where sits bright Liberty.

    [ Recitative]

    Otoo :

    57 The splendid vision, oh, my son obey,

    58 Towha commands for England quick away.

    59 But ere, lov'd youth, you quit your native shore,

    60 Your rival Oediddee shall restore

    61 The Royal Ensigns, Britain to convince

    62 That in Omai she receives a Prince.

    Scene 2.

    [ Inside of a Morai of the antient Aree-de-hy's [9] Grand Chiefs Ancestors of Omai. ]

    [ Enter Otoo and Omai]

    [ Recitative]

    Otoo :

    [ Omai kneels.]

    63 Alas! to spare him you in vain implore me;

    64 Quick, fly and bring Oediddee here before me.

    [ Recitative]

    Oediddee :

    65 Oh, sage Otoo! accept my true contrition,

    66 I yield obedience to each hard condition;

    67 Your son still loves me, still I love that son,

    68 From childhood still the race of life we've run;

    69 My smallest grief was anguish to his heart,

    70 His slightest joy could every joy impart.

    [ Air IV]

    Oediddee :

    71 O'er groves of coral, through the deep,

    72 Where Mogee [10] An excellent species of fish in those seas. fish their revel keep,

    73 Our staunch canoes in consort glide,

    74 Tho'stern Farooa [11] A strong and dangerous wind swells the tide,

    75 Small the danger each could see,

    76 Friendship cried, you're safe with me.

    77 On the hill, that upward towers,

    78 Crown'd with shrubs and sweetest flowers,

    79 There we taste eternal spring,

    80 While the Wattle-bird [12] The most melodious in the Islands. shall sing

    81 Double joys to him and me,

    82 Ever such let friendship be.

    [ Recitative]

    Otoo :

    83 Resign, fond youth, each silly vain pretension.

    Oediddee :

    84 What good awaits my tacit condescension?

    Otoo :

    85 Your fortunes with my son I'll link for ever

    86 So strong, that nought but death your fates can sever.

    87 Surrounding isles shall own thy sovereign sway,

    88 E'en distant Wateoo [13] The farthest situated from Otaheite. shall tribute pay,

    89 Be you their Chief, their powerful Areekee [14] King.

    90 'T' Omai leave th' Otaheitean crown, this is Otoo's decree.

    91 To Britain, now, Omai must repair,

    92 And many dangers run to win the British fair.

    [ Air V]

    Oediddee :

    93 Adieu, dear Omai, and gladly receive

    94 That true and kind welcome that Britons can give.

    [ General Chorus]

    General Chorus :

    95 Adieu, Prince Omai, and gladly receive

    96 That true and kind welcome that Britons can give.

    Scene 3.

    [ A view of Plymouth Sound, with Part of Mount Edgcumb, where Omai, with Harlequin as his servant, lands; as also does Don Struttolando, with Clown as his Servant: both in pursuit of the Object of their Affections, Londina. Different comic Business is here introduced, with the two following Airs by an old Water-cress Woman, or Fairy. ]

    [ Air VI]

    Old Fairy :

    97 Cold and hungry tho' I sing

    98 Water-cresses o'the spring;

    99 Your money, neighbours, little less is,

    100 So buy my nice spring water-cresses;

    101 Buy my nice spring water-cresses.

    [ Air VII]

    Old Fairy :

    102 Keep it, prithee keep it, my kind, my generous Boy;

    103 And featly, nimbly use it, shou'd surly care annoy.

    104 Quick posting on a sun0beam, here potent Towha sent me;

    105 His charge "take care of Harlequin" and pow'rful spells he lent me.

    106 Be still Omai's valet, he

    107 With kind regard

    108 Shall well reward

    109 Thy care and strict fidelity.

    110 Let pity touch thy bosom thro' frolick and vagary,

    111 And though shalt ever find me thy true, thy guardian fairy.

    [ The Scene ends with Omai's losing his Talisman, and their going off to a Justice of Peace for its recovery. ]
